Traditional Dance Description

Mokorotlo Dance

Performed by men wearing the iconic straw hat, the Mokorotlo dance involves rhythmic foot-stamping and high-knee lifts. The beat is driven by the lekolulo flute and hand-claps, symbolising strength and gratitude to ancestors.

litolobonya

Young girls and boys celebrate the end of initiation school with Litolobonya. Dancers form a semicircle, swaying colourful seshoeshoe skirts while singing call-and-response songs that teach social values and clan history.

Mokhibo

Mokhibo embodies the storytelling tradition of the Basotho people. It portrays their history, legends, and values through rhythmic movements and gestures.

Tsilo

This dance is performed in circular formations and symbolizes unity and love among the Basotho community.

Ndlamo

This vibrant dance is popular across Lesotho and is often featured in traditional weedings.It si characterised by enegertic movements and is a celebration of joy and community.

Liphotha

Perfomed by men, especially warriors or herdboys.Enegertic movements that include jumping and clapping and it celebrate bravery or community gatherings.

Mohobelo

Male dance perfomed with stomping and kicking, shows strength, pride and coordination It is often perfomed during cultural events and ceremonies.

Selia-lia

It is a dance done by both boys and girls while chosing their loved once but in a form of a game.

Lipina tsa mokopu

This is a dance done by girls wearing "thethana"and they dance running in a circle singing.

Maqekha

This is a dance done by women during their initiation while wearing goat skin dresses

khokho

A dance done by women shaking their bodies.

Music Instruments Descriptions

Lesiba

The lesiba consists of a flattened quill attached to a long piece of sinew string, which is itself stretched over a piece of hardwood. The wood acts as the resonating surface for the string. However – unlike most stringed instruments – what is unique about the lesiba is that its string is not plucked, bowed or struck in any way, but rather resonated by the player’s mouth.

Lekolulo

This is a flute-like instrument traditionally played by herding boys. It is made from local materials and is integral to Basotho music, often used in storytelling and cultural expressions.

Setolo-tolo

Resembling a jaw harp, the setolo-tolo is played by men using their mouths. It adds a distinctive rhythmic element to traditional music and is often used in communal gatherings.

Mamokhorong

Also known as sekhankula, this instrument has gained popularity in recent years and is used in various musical contexts within Basotho culture.

Moropa

A type of flute that contributes to the haunting melodies characteristic of Basotho music, often used in conjunction with other instruments during performances.

Thomo

The string is sruck or plugged while the mouth changes the pitch or resonance.

Lengope

Wooden or metal clapping sticks used to create percussive sounds.

Motlolobonya

Made od dried seed pods worn by dancers and a dancer moves or stomps the material rattle rhythmically, adding sounds to the performance.

Liphala

Animal horns blown to make lound sounds and it is used in ceremonies, traditional gatherings or by chief to call attention.

Khantsi

Triditional whistle made froom reed or bone.It is used by herdboys or in ceremonies for signaling or rhythm.

Lefika la mololi

A flat bone with holes blown to produce whistle sound.

Lekolulo

Lehlaka used to make whistling sound.
